What is Decomposition?

Decomposition is the process of breaking down a problem into smaller sub-problems. Instead of solving everything at once, we focus on solving one part at a time. This approach is widely used in programming, science, and even everyday life!

Real-Life Examples from Indonesia

Family Trip to Bali

Break down into finding flights, booking hotels, making itinerary, and packing essentials.

Cooking Rendang

Steps include preparing ingredients, making spice paste, slow cooking, and final seasoning.

School Event Organization

Divide responsibilities among decoration, budgeting, logistics, and marketing teams.

Why is Decomposition Important?

Improves Focus

Concentrate on one part at a time without feeling stressed.

Enhances Collaboration

Allows teams to work simultaneously on different parts.

Easier Debugging

Fix issues in small parts without redoing everything.

Faster Solutions

Solve complex problems in manageable chunks.
