What is Computational Thinking?

Computational Thinking is a method of problem-solving that involves breaking down complex problems into smaller parts, recognizing patterns, abstracting unnecessary details, and creating step-by-step solutions. It's not just for computer scientists‚Äîit's a valuable skill for everyone!

Methods of Computational Thinking

Decomposition

Breaking down a complex problem into manageable pieces.

Pattern Recognition

Identifying trends and commonalities to simplify problems.

Abstraction

Focusing on the important details while ignoring the irrelevant ones.

Algorithm Design

Developing a clear, step-by-step strategy to solve problems efficiently.
